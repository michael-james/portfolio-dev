my story

My work engages play, empathy, and analytics to devise solutions to wicked problems. I spend time with people, tools, and ideas until I know their truth. I use these discoveries to create human-centered services and environments, with an eye towards emerging technologies such as machine learning, VR & AR, and ambient displays. My theatre arts and interaction design practices inform each other on a daily basis.

I graduate from Carnegie Mellon University in May, where I'm studying Video & Media Design for Drama and Human-Computer Interaction. I've done research in homelessness, data visualization, and mental health. I've designed and devised performance work with students at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland and presented at the Prague Quadrennial.
